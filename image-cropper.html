What This Tool Does

Cropping removes everything outside a rectangle you draw on the picture, leaving only the region you selected. It's useful for cutting out a subject, removing unwanted edges, or changing an image's proportions.

Steps to Crop a Picture

  1. Load a picture into the box above
  2. Click and drag directly on the picture to draw a selection rectangle
  3. Adjust the box by drawing a new one if needed
  4. Click Crop, then Download the cropped result

What You Can Control

  • Freeform drag-to-select cropping directly on the picture
  • A live readout of the selected width and height in pixels
  • Redraw the selection as many times as you like before cropping
  • Output keeps the original file format
  • No part of the image leaves your browser tab

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I crop to an exact aspect ratio like 1:1?

This tool uses a freeform rectangle rather than fixed ratio presets, so you draw the box by hand and can approximate a square or any other shape by watching the width and height readout.

What happens if my selection is too small?

Selections under 5 by 5 pixels are ignored so you don't accidentally crop away the entire picture.

Does cropping reduce image quality?

No. The kept pixels are copied exactly as they were, with no re-compression beyond the export format you already had.

Can I undo a crop?

Click Start Over to reload the original file and begin again with a fresh selection.

Does this work on a touchscreen?

The selection is driven by mouse drag events, so a trackpad or mouse gives the most precise control. Touch support depends on your browser.

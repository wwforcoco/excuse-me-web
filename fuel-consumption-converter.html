Convert Fuel Economy Figures

Fuel economy is reported differently around the world: Europe typically uses liters per 100 kilometers (L/100km), the US uses miles per gallon (MPG), the UK uses its own imperial MPG, and some countries use kilometers per liter (km/L). Because L/100km measures fuel used per distance while MPG and km/L measure distance per fuel, converting between them isn't a simple multiplication — this tool handles the math correctly.

Enter a fuel economy figure, choose your source and target units, and get an accurate converted value instantly. Useful when comparing a car's specifications between US, UK, and metric-market listings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn't converting MPG to L/100km a simple multiplication?

L/100km measures fuel used per fixed distance, while MPG measures distance traveled per unit of fuel — they're inversely related, so the conversion requires dividing rather than simply scaling.

Is US MPG the same as UK MPG?

No. A US gallon (3.785 L) is smaller than a UK imperial gallon (4.546 L), so the same MPG figure means a different real-world fuel economy in each country. This converter treats them separately.

What does a lower L/100km number mean?

A lower L/100km value means better fuel economy, since it represents less fuel used to travel 100 kilometers.

What does a higher MPG number mean?

A higher MPG value means better fuel economy, since the vehicle travels farther per gallon of fuel.

Can I convert km/L to MPG?

Yes, select kilometers per liter as the source unit and either US or UK miles per gallon as the target.

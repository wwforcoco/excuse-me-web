What Is an Online Calculator?

A calculator is an electronic or software device designed to execute mathematical computations. It interprets numerical inputs, applies selected operators, and outputs accurate results within milliseconds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What operations can this calculator handle?

It covers the four basic operations — addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division — plus percentage calculations, all through on-screen buttons or your keyboard.

Can I type on my keyboard instead of clicking the buttons?

Yes. Number keys, +, −, ×, ÷, and % all work directly from your keyboard. Press Enter for equals, Backspace to delete the last character, and Escape to clear everything.

Does it handle decimals and negative numbers?

Yes. The decimal point button supports fractional values, and you can enter negative numbers by starting with a minus sign.

Does it follow the standard order of operations?

Yes. When you chain operations together, such as 4 + 2 × 3, the calculator applies standard order of operations rather than calculating strictly left to right.

Is my data stored or sent anywhere?

No. All calculations run locally in your browser — nothing is transmitted to a server, saved to an account, or stored after you close the page.

Does it work on mobile devices?

Yes, the layout is fully responsive and works the same way on phones, tablets, and desktop browsers.

What if I make a mistake while entering numbers?

Use Backspace, or the on-screen backspace button, to remove the last character, or AC to clear the whole calculation and start over.

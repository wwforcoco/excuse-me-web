Frequently Asked Questions

How long should my bio be?

It depends on the platform:
• Instagram: 150 characters visible before "...more"
• X/Twitter: 160 characters
• LinkedIn: 2,600 characters (headline + experience)
• TikTok: Only first ~80 characters before truncation

The best bios are concise but complete — they tell people who you are and what you do in one or two sentences.

What's the difference between a good bio and a bad one?

A good bio has:
• A hook (first 5 words grab attention)
• Context (what you do / your unique angle)
• Personality (tone that matches your brand)

A bad bio is:
• Generic filler like "Digital nomad 🌍"
• Overused phrases everyone uses ("Love to travel ✈️")
• No indication of what makes you different from competitors
• Pure buzzwords without substance behind them

Should I use emojis in my professional bio?

Use them sparingly in conservative fields (law, finance, medicine).
Use more freely in creative fields (design, marketing, food, art).
Match the tone expectations of your specific profession.
Avoid emojis entirely if you're in a formal industry where they're considered unprofessional.

Can I use these for dating apps?

Absolutely! Choose "Playful" or "Creative" vibe.
Dating app bios work best when they:
• Show humor and personality
Hint at interests beyond looks
Avoid being overly generic ("I like having fun")

Do I need to update my bio regularly?

Only refresh it when:
• You change jobs or careers
You rebrand or pivot your niche
Your current bio no longer represents you
You notice it feels stale or outdated

Otherwise, bios can stay valid for months without updates. Refresh quarterly at most unless something significant changes in your life.

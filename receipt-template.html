What Is a Receipt Template?

A receipt confirms that a payment has already been made — unlike an invoice, which requests payment. Use it to give a customer proof of purchase, or to keep your own record of a transaction that's complete.

How to Use This Template

  1. Fill in the fields on the left — your entries appear in the live preview.
  2. Click "Update Preview" any time you change a field.
  3. Click directly inside the preview to fine-tune wording, spacing, or line breaks.
  4. Download as a text file, or use Print → Save as PDF for a polished document.

Tips

  • Always include the payment method (cash, card, transfer) so both sides have a clear record.
  • Give receipts a unique number if you issue more than a handful — it makes future lookups easier.
  • Issue a receipt immediately after payment is received, not days later.
  • Keep a copy for your own bookkeeping, especially for tax-deductible purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between this and the Invoice Template?

An invoice requests payment that's still owed. A receipt confirms payment that has already been received. Use whichever matches where you are in the transaction.

Does the total add up automatically?

Yes — enter items as Description | Qty | Price and the total calculates when you click Update Preview.

Can I use this for a personal sale, not just a business?

Yes, it works for any transaction — business sales, freelance work, or a one-off personal sale.

How do I hand this to a customer?

Download it as a .txt file for quick copy-paste, or use Print → Save as PDF for a polished, printable copy.

What Is a Project Report Template?

A project report gives stakeholders a snapshot of where a project stands right now — what's been completed, what's getting in the way, and what happens next. It's typically sent weekly, biweekly, or at key milestones.

How to Use This Template

  1. Fill in the fields on the left — your entries appear in the live preview.
  2. Click "Update Preview" any time you change a field.
  3. Click directly inside the preview to fine-tune wording, spacing, or line breaks.
  4. Download as a text file, or use Print → Save as PDF for a polished document.

Tips

  • Lead with a one-line overall status (on track, at risk, delayed) before the details.
  • Be upfront about challenges — a report that hides problems is less useful than one that surfaces them early.
  • Keep progress updates specific: what shipped, not just what was 'worked on'.
  • List next steps as concrete actions with rough timing, not vague intentions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I send a project report?

Weekly or biweekly is common for active projects; align the cadence to how often stakeholders need visibility.

How is this different from a Project Proposal?

A proposal is written before a project to pitch and plan it. This report is written during or after to summarize actual progress.

Can I include a budget status?

Yes, there's a dedicated field for a short budget status note alongside progress and challenges.

Can I download this to email to stakeholders?

Yes, download as .txt for quick pasting into an email, or Print → Save as PDF for a formatted attachment.

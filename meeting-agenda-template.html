What Is a Meeting Agenda Template?

A meeting agenda sets expectations before a meeting starts — what will be discussed, who's presenting each topic, and how long each item should take. Sending one out in advance keeps meetings on schedule and gives attendees time to prepare.

How to Use This Template

  1. Fill in the fields on the left — your entries appear in the live preview.
  2. Click "Update Preview" any time you change a field.
  3. Click directly inside the preview to fine-tune wording, spacing, or line breaks.
  4. Download as a text file, or use Print → Save as PDF for a polished document.

Tips

  • Send the agenda at least a day in advance so attendees can prepare.
  • Assign a time limit to each topic to keep the meeting on track.
  • Name an owner for each agenda item, not just the meeting as a whole.
  • List the meeting objective at the top so everyone knows the intended outcome.
  • Keep the agenda to 5-7 items — long agendas rarely get fully covered.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many agenda items should a meeting have?

Aim for 5 to 7 focused items — more than that usually means the meeting should be split in two.

Can I assign time limits to each topic?

Yes, enter Topic | Presenter | Duration for each line and it renders as a structured table.

What's the difference between this and Meeting Minutes?

This agenda is prepared before the meeting to plan it. The separate Meeting Minutes Template is filled in during or after the meeting to record what actually happened.

Can I download this to share with attendees?

Yes — download as .txt for a quick copy-paste into an email, or use Print → Save as PDF for a formatted document.

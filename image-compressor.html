Image Compressor

Shrink a picture's file size with an adjustable quality slider, then compare before and after.

Drop a picture here, or click to choose one
Works with JPG, PNG, and WebP

What This Tool Does

This tool re-encodes a picture at a lower quality setting so the file takes up less storage space and loads faster on a page. It keeps the pixel dimensions the same and only changes how tightly the colors are compressed.

Steps to Shrink a File

  1. Load a JPG, PNG, or WebP file into the box above
  2. Move the quality slider until the preview looks acceptable to you
  3. Watch the size counter update as you adjust the slider
  4. Click Compress, then Download to save the smaller file

What You Can Control

  • A quality slider from 10 to 100 with a live estimated output size
  • Side-by-side comparison of the starting size and the new size
  • Option to output as JPG or WebP, both of which compress better than PNG for photos
  • No pixel dimensions are altered, only the compression level
  • Processing happens on your device, not on a remote server

Frequently Asked Questions

How much smaller can a file get?

It depends on the picture and the quality you choose. Photos with lots of fine detail compress less than simple graphics, but a drop from 100 to 70 quality often cuts the size by more than half.

Will lowering quality make my picture look worse?

At very low settings you may notice blocky patches or color banding. Settings between 60 and 85 usually keep the picture looking clean while still shrinking the file.

Why is PNG output not shrinking much?

PNG uses lossless compression, so there is no quality slider to trade against size. For a smaller file, export as JPG or WebP instead.

Can I compress a picture I already resized?

Yes. Resize it first with the resizer tool, then bring the result here to compress it further.

Is there a limit to how many pictures I can compress?

No. You can compress as many files as you like, one at a time, with no daily cap.

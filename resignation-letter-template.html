What Is a Resignation Letter Template?

A resignation letter formally notifies your employer that you're leaving, states your last working day, and keeps the door open for a good reference. This template covers the essentials without over-explaining your reasons.

  3. Click directly inside the preview to fine-tune wording, spacing, or line breaks.
  4. Download as a text file, or use Print → Save as PDF for a polished document.

Tips

  • State your last working day clearly and near the top — that's the detail HR needs first.
  • Keep the tone professional and brief; you don't owe a detailed explanation of why you're leaving.
  • Offer to help with the transition — it leaves a stronger final impression.
  • Give standard notice (commonly two weeks) unless your contract specifies otherwise.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much notice should I give?

Two weeks is standard in many places, but check your contract or company policy — some roles or regions expect more.

Do I need to explain why I'm resigning?

No. A brief, professional letter without a detailed reason is completely acceptable and often preferable.

Should I mention my next job?

It's optional and usually unnecessary — the letter's job is to formally document your departure, not detail your next move.

How do I deliver this letter?

Download as a PDF using Print → Save as PDF, then hand it to your manager or attach it to an email as your employer prefers.

What Is a Tip Calculator?

A tip calculator determines appropriate gratuity amounts based on service bill totals and cultural percentage norms. It further divides the complete obligation (meal plus tip) among multiple diners to ensure equitable financial contribution.

Common Uses

  • Calculating restaurant gratuity after group dining experiences
  • Determining fair compensation for food delivery personnel
  • Splitting hospitality bills among friends with different budget constraints
  • Estimating service charges for salon, spa, and personal care appointments
  • Budgeting entertainment expenses with accurate tip projections

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Preset buttons immediately apply 10%, 15%, 18%, 20%, or 25% gratuity rates
  • Enter custom percentages for specialized service contexts
  • Adjust the people counter to split obligations evenly or unevenly
  • Results update in real-time as you modify the bill amount
  • Round up to the nearest dollar for cleaner cash transactions

Pro Tips

  • In the United States, 15-20% represents standard restaurant gratuity for satisfactory service
  • Tip delivery drivers 10-15% or a minimum of $2-3 regardless of order size
  • For large parties (8+), verify whether gratuity was already included in the check total
  • Bar service typically warrants $1-2 per drink or 15-20% of the total tab
  • When service falls below expectations, address concerns with management rather than withholding tip entirely

Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage should I tip at restaurants?

Most diners leave 15-20% for standard service, 25%+ for exceptional experiences, and 10% for subpar encounters.

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?

Etiquette generally suggests calculating gratuity on the pre-tax subtotal, though many tip on the total for convenience.

How do I split a bill when some people ordered more expensive items?

This tool divides evenly. For itemized splits, request separate checks or use a spreadsheet approach.

Is tipping expected for takeout orders?

Not traditionally required, though 10% or a few dollars acknowledges kitchen staff preparation effort.

What about tipping in other countries?

Tipping customs vary dramatically. Research local norms before traveling, as some cultures view gratuity as insulting.

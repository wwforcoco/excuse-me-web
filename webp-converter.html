What This Tool Does

WebP is a modern format that generally produces smaller files than JPG or PNG at a similar visual quality, which is why many websites prefer it. This tool converts any image you load into WebP, or takes a WebP file and outputs it as JPG or PNG for programs that don't support WebP.

Steps to Convert

  1. Load any JPG, PNG, or WebP file into the box above
  2. Choose the output format you need: WebP, JPG, or PNG
  3. If converting to WebP or JPG, set the quality level
  4. Click Convert, then Download the result

Why Use WebP

  • Typically 25-35% smaller than a JPG of similar visual quality
  • Supports transparency, unlike JPG
  • Works both directions: convert into WebP or out of it
  • Adjustable quality for the WebP and JPG output options
  • Everything runs locally in your browser tab

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all browsers support WebP?

Every major modern browser displays WebP images. Some older image editors and a few legacy systems still expect JPG or PNG, which is why the reverse conversion is offered here too.

Is WebP always smaller than JPG?

Usually, but not in every case. For some pictures, especially simple graphics, PNG or WebP lossless can end up smaller than a compressed JPG.

Does WebP support transparency like PNG?

Yes, WebP supports an alpha channel, so transparent areas from a PNG are preserved when converting to WebP.

Can I convert a WebP file into a JPG?

Yes, select JPG as the output format after loading your WebP file, and choose a background color if the source has transparency.

Will converting to WebP lower my picture quality?

At high quality settings the difference is usually not visible. Lower settings trade some detail for a smaller file, same as JPG.

What Is the Memory Game?

The Memory Game, also known as Concentration, is a card matching game played with a grid of face-down cards. Flip two cards at a time, and if their symbols match, they stay face up. If not, they flip back over, so remembering each card's location and symbol is key to clearing the board quickly.

How to Play

  • Tap any face-down card to reveal its symbol
  • Tap a second card to see if the two symbols match
  • Matched pairs stay face up; unmatched pairs flip back over after a short pause
  • Clear every pair on the board to finish the round

Strategy Tips

  • Build a mental map of the grid instead of flipping cards at random
  • When you reveal a new symbol, remember its position in case you see its match later
  • Work through unexplored cards first before re-checking ones you've already seen
  • Start on Easy to build the habit, then move to Medium or Hard as your recall improves

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I play the memory game?

Tap two cards to flip them. If the symbols match, the pair stays face up. If they don't match, both cards flip back so you can try again.

What do the difficulty levels change?

Easy uses 12 cards (6 pairs), Medium uses 16 cards (8 pairs), and Hard uses 20 cards (10 pairs), each adding more symbols to remember.

How is my performance measured?

The game tracks your move count and elapsed time, so you can try to clear the board in fewer moves or less time on your next attempt.

Can I restart at any point?

Yes, press New Game at any time to reshuffle the board and start a fresh round at your current difficulty.

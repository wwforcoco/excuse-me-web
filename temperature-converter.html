Frequently Asked Questions

What is the formula to convert Celsius to Fahrenheit?

Fahrenheit equals Celsius multiplied by 9/5, plus 32. The converter applies this automatically.

What temperature is 0 Kelvin?

0 Kelvin is absolute zero, equal to -273.15°C or -459.67°F, the coldest theoretically possible temperature.

Is 100°C always 212°F?

Yes, 100°C (water's boiling point at sea level) converts exactly to 212°F.

Can I enter negative temperatures?

Yes, the converter accepts negative values for Celsius and Fahrenheit as well as values below zero Kelvin, though Kelvin has no negative values in physical reality.

Why does the US use Fahrenheit while most countries use Celsius?

It's largely historical convention; the US retained the Fahrenheit scale for everyday use while most of the world adopted the metric-based Celsius scale.

Yes, it's completely free and requires no sign-up or account.

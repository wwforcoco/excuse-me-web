What Is 2048?

2048 is a single-player sliding tile puzzle played on a 4×4 grid. Every move slides all tiles as far as possible in the chosen direction, and any two tiles that touch with the same number combine into one tile with double the value. The game adds a new tile (2 or 4) after each move, creating increasing pressure as the board fills up.

How to Play

  1. Click the game board once to ensure it has focus (you'll see a blue highlight)
  2. Press Arrow Keys (↑ ↓ ← →) or WASD to slide all tiles simultaneously in one direction
  3. Watch tiles merge — when two tiles with the same number collide, they combine into one tile worth double the value (e.g., two 4s become an 8)
  4. A new tile appears randomly after each move (90% chance it's a 2, 10% chance it's a 4)
  5. Keep merging until you create the 2048 tile — or go further if you can!
  6. The game ends when the board is full and no adjacent tiles can merge

Strategy Tips

  • Corner Strategy: Pick one corner (usually bottom-right) and keep your highest-value tile there permanently. Never move it away.
  • Build in One Direction: Try to fill rows sequentially. Don't scatter high-value tiles across the entire board.
  • Avoid Wasted Moves: Only press a key if it creates a merge or sets up a future merge. Random shuffling fills the board faster.
  • Plan Two Moves Ahead: Before pressing Left, think "After this, will I be able to press Down safely?"
  • The Snake Pattern: Advanced players build tiles in a snaking pattern (fill bottom row left-to-right, then row above right-to-left, etc.)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep playing after reaching 2048?

Absolutely! The game doesn't stop at 2048. You can continue playing to build 4096, 8192, 16384, and beyond. Many players treat 2048 as just the first milestone rather than the end goal. Your score keeps climbing as long as you can make moves.

Does the game work on mobile phones?

Yes, fully optimized for touch screens. Swipe in any direction on the game board to move tiles. The touch detection is tuned to ignore accidental light touches while responding to deliberate swipes. Works on iPhone Safari, Android Chrome, iPads, and tablets.

Is my score saved if I close the browser?

Your personal best score is saved automatically in your browser's local storage. It persists even after closing the tab, restarting the browser, or shutting down your computer. The only way to lose it is if you manually clear your browser data/cache or use incognito/private browsing mode.

What is the highest possible tile in 2048?

Theoretically, the maximum tile is 131,072 (which is 2^17), but reaching it requires nearly perfect play over thousands of moves. For most players, reaching 2048 takes practice, 4096 is impressive, and 8192 is expert-level. Our game displays any value correctly no matter how high you go.

Why do I sometimes get a 4 tile instead of 2?

The game randomly spawns either a 2 (90% probability) or a 4 (10% probability) after each move. Getting more 4s earlier in the game actually helps you reach higher scores faster, since you're starting with bigger building blocks. It's purely luck — there's no skill involved in which number appears.

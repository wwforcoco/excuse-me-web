BMI Calculator Formula

BMI is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in meters squared (BMI = kg / m²). In imperial units, BMI equals weight in pounds multiplied by 703, divided by height in inches squared.

What Is a BMI Calculator?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening metric that divides body mass by the square of stature. It categorizes individuals into underweight, normal, overweight, or obese classifications based on population health data.

Common Uses

  • Screening for potential weight-related health risks during physical exams
  • Setting fitness targets with measurable numerical benchmarks
  • Tracking body composition changes throughout diet and exercise programs
  • Providing baseline data for nutritionist consultations
  • Evaluating athletic readiness and performance weight classes

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Toggle between metric and imperial units with a single button click
  • Enter height in feet and inches separately for imperial measurements
  • The visual gauge immediately displays your category upon calculation
  • Tab between input fields using the keyboard for faster entry
  • Results round to one decimal place for clinical precision

Pro Tips

  • BMI serves as a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument for body fat percentage
  • Athletes with high muscle mass may register as overweight despite low body fat
  • Children and teenagers require age-specific BMI percentile charts
  • Combine BMI with waist circumference measurements for more comprehensive health assessment
  • Consult healthcare providers before making significant lifestyle changes based on BMI alone

BMI Limitations

BMI does not distinguish between muscle, bone, and fat mass, so it can misclassify very muscular people as overweight and underestimate risk in people with low muscle mass. It also does not account for fat distribution, which matters for cardiovascular risk, or for factors like frame size, pregnancy, and ethnicity-specific health thresholds.

BMI for Women and Men

The standard BMI formula and category thresholds are the same for adult women and men, since BMI does not adjust for sex-based differences in body composition. Women naturally tend to carry a higher percentage of body fat than men at the same BMI, which is one reason many clinicians pair BMI with waist circumference or body fat percentage for a fuller picture.

BMI for Children

Standard adult BMI categories do not apply to children and teenagers. Pediatric BMI is plotted on age- and sex-specific growth charts as a percentile compared to other children of the same age, rather than using the fixed underweight/normal/overweight/obese cutoffs used for adults.

BMI vs Body Fat Percentage

BMI is a simple ratio of weight to height and is easy to calculate from two measurements, but it does not directly measure fat. Body fat percentage, measured through methods like skinfold calipers, bioelectrical impedance, or DEXA scans, more accurately reflects how much of a person's weight is fat versus lean tissue, making it a useful complement to BMI rather than a replacement for it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What BMI range indicates healthy weight?

Adults generally fall within healthy parameters between 18.5 and 24.9.

Why does my BMI differ between metric and imperial?

It should not differ. Both formulas produce identical results when converted accurately.

Can BMI accurately assess bodybuilders?

No. Dense muscle tissue weighs more than fat, potentially skewing results toward overweight categories.

What is the formula used here?

Metric: weight(kg) ÷ height(m)². Imperial: 703 × weight(lbs) ÷ height(in)².

Should elderly individuals use standard BMI ranges?

Older adults may benefit from slightly higher BMI ranges (23-27) as some extra weight provides protective benefits against illness.

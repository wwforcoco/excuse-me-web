Image Rotator

Turn a sideways photo the right way up, or set a custom rotation angle.

Drop a picture here, or click to choose one
JPG, PNG, or WebP

What This Tool Does

Photos taken with a phone held sideways, or scanned pages fed in wrong, often need to be turned before they look correct. This tool spins the picture in 90 degree steps or to any exact angle you set, and redraws the canvas so the rotated result is what gets saved.

Steps to Rotate a Picture

  1. Load a picture into the box above
  2. Click Rotate Left or Rotate Right to turn it a quarter turn at a time
  3. Or drag the angle slider for a precise custom rotation
  4. Click Download once the picture looks right

What You Can Control

  • One-click 90 degree turns in either direction
  • A free-angle slider from -180 to 180 degrees for fine adjustments
  • The canvas automatically expands so nothing gets clipped at odd angles
  • Keeps the source format for the downloaded file
  • Runs entirely in your browser with no upload step

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the canvas size change after rotating?

At angles other than 0, 90, 180, or 270 degrees, a rotated rectangle needs a larger bounding box to fit without cutting off corners, so the canvas expands to hold the whole picture.

Can I rotate by an exact number like 15 degrees?

Yes, the angle slider lets you pick any whole-degree value in that range.

Will rotating fill the empty corners with a color?

The corners left empty by a non-90-degree rotation are transparent for PNG or WebP output, or white for JPG output.

Does rotating affect picture quality?

A 90, 180, or 270 degree turn does not lose any detail. Other angles involve some pixel resampling, similar to resizing.

Can I rotate a picture back after saving it?

Yes, load the downloaded file back into this tool and rotate it the opposite direction.

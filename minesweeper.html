What Is Minesweeper?

Minesweeper is a logic puzzle played on a grid of hidden cells, some of which contain mines. Revealing a safe cell shows a number indicating how many mines touch it, and you use those numbers to deduce which nearby cells are safe and which to flag as mines.

How to Play

  • Tap or click a cell to reveal it; numbers show how many mines surround that cell
  • Right-click, or long-press on mobile, to flag a cell you believe hides a mine
  • Use the numbers on revealed cells to work out where it is safe to click next
  • Clear every non-mine cell on the board to win

Strategy Tips

  • Start by clearing open areas with no adjacent mines, which reveal in one tap
  • Compare overlapping numbers to find cells that must be safe or must be mines
  • Flag cells you are certain about first, then use flags to reason about neighboring numbers
  • Move to the Medium or Hard board once you consistently clear Easy boards

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I flag a suspected mine?

Right-click a cell on desktop, or press and hold a cell on a touchscreen, to place or remove a flag without revealing it.

What happens on my first click?

The first cell you reveal is always guaranteed to be safe, and mines are placed after that click so you never lose on the opening move.

How do I win?

You win by revealing every cell that does not contain a mine, without ever clicking directly on a mine.

Can I change the board size and difficulty?

Yes, use the Easy, Medium, or Hard buttons above the board to switch grid size and mine count before starting a new game.

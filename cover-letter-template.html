What Is a Cover Letter Template?

A cover letter accompanies your resume and gives you room to explain, in your own words, why you're applying and why you'd be a good fit. It's typically three to four short paragraphs: an opening that names the role, a middle that connects your experience to the job, and a closing that asks for an interview.

Tips

  • Address a specific person whenever you can find a name instead of 'To Whom It May Concern'.
  • Open by naming the exact role and where you saw it — avoid a generic first line.
  • Connect one or two concrete achievements to what the job posting asks for.
  • Keep the whole letter under one page — hiring managers skim quickly.
  • End with a clear, confident request for an interview or next step.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a cover letter if I already have a resume?

Many job postings request one, and it's a chance to explain fit and enthusiasm in ways a resume's bullet points can't. Include one whenever it's requested or optional but easy to add.

How long should a cover letter be?

Aim for three to four short paragraphs — enough to make your case without repeating your entire resume.

Is this different from the Job Application Letter template?

Yes. This cover letter is written to accompany a resume for a specific posting. The Job Application Letter template is a more formal, standalone letter often used when applying directly without a separate resume attachment.

Can I reuse the same letter for multiple jobs?

You can reuse the structure, but change the company name, role, and specific examples each time — generic letters are easy to spot.

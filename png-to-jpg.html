What This Tool Does

JPG has no concept of transparency, so any see-through pixels in a PNG need a solid color behind them before conversion. This tool lets you pick that fill color, then re-encodes the picture as a JPG at a quality level you control.

Steps to Convert

  1. Choose a PNG file from the box above
  2. Pick a background color to fill any transparent parts of the picture
  3. Set the JPG quality level with the slider
  4. Click Convert, then Download the .jpg file

Why Convert to JPG

  • JPG files are usually far smaller than PNG for the same photo
  • A custom background color fills transparent regions cleanly
  • Adjustable quality lets you balance sharpness against file size
  • Widely compatible with older software and every browser
  • No part of the picture is sent to a server during conversion

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to transparent areas of my PNG?

They are filled with the background color you choose before the JPG is created, since JPG cannot store transparency.

What color should I pick if I am not sure?

White is the safest default for most photos and documents.

Will converting reduce the picture quality?

JPG uses lossy compression, so some fine detail is discarded. Keeping the quality slider above 80 usually keeps the difference hard to notice.

Can I convert a screenshot with text this way?

Yes, though PNG is generally the better format for text-heavy images since JPG compression can blur sharp edges at lower quality settings.

Does this tool resize my picture?

No, the pixel dimensions stay the same. Use the image resizer separately if you also want to change the size.
